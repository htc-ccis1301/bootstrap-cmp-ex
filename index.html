Bootstrap Component Examples

Nav Bar

Look at the navbar HTML using either the browser Developer Tools. Notice that my navbar is much simpler (smaller) than the fuller example on the Bootstrap site. Their example is meant to show you variety in what you can do, so feel free to strip out parts you may not need. I removed the dropdown navigation, the form, and the navbar-right.

Make sure to also reduce the browser window's width to simulate the mobile phone and notice the hamburger nav button - the one that has 3 bars in it. Click that to show and hide the navigation on a mobile site. While many User Experience (UX) people have argued this is not intuitive for users, once shown what it does, users learn and since it is now common across many sites this is less of an issue.

The other thing worth noting in the navbar example is the active link which is highlighted in grey and also includes an invisible helper style for sr-only (screen readers only). This aids in accessibility and you will see it throughout many of the Bootstrap examples.

This can make a nice image gallery, or it could be used as a rotating main display (jumbotron-like) image at the top of your page. The captions are optional, and you can also include the images as image HTML elements instead of background CSS images. Play around with the carousel content and it can also include links, either as the entire image, or as the caption text.
